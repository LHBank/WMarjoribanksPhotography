A Career Change? Full Stack SolutionBlog Post Heading

This article takes a slightly different theme, when considering past entries. It delves into the reason behind my career change – final change more importantly – of Full Stack Web Development.

I’ve worn many hats, figuratively and literally speaking, throughout my career. At a young age, I voluntarily served my community as a Special Police Constable, followed by various customer service roles, farming, taxi-driving, studying toward a Diploma in Dental Technology, and more recently, teaching English as a foreign language.

Here I am now. In the midst of uncertainty, with millions of others during this time of crisis. Everyone’s either wearing a mask, or refusing to based on constitutional rights or personal opinion. It’s changing the way we socialise, the way we work and how we get from point A to point B.

What it doesn’t change is the shortage of web developers there will be, by next year. By published report, on ncube.com, there are going to be 1.4million positions in demand, to fulfil the needs of society in the US alone.

So why doesn’t everyone do it? Well for one, unless you enjoy being at a computer for 8+ hours a day, not to mention inevitable overtime, it might not float your boat. Unless you find solace in problem-solving, to reveal you forgot to separate your class’ from one another, or a relative path wasn't used for an image link, it might not be for you. This website was developed and designed by me, after only seven weeks of study, and guidance from an attributed mentor from Code Institute, Dublin.

You may think the turnaround time is pretty good then? Sure, however this is just is the beginning and I am by no means an expert. As with any skill, practice is imperative. The languages this site is based upon, HTML and CSS, aren’t overly mathematical - which is fortunate for me, as you may have determined yourself, I’m more inclined to the creative. Javascript and Python though – challenge accepted...? Algebraic by nature, I envision myself needing many “Goosfraba” moments already.

Does this deter me – no. Of course not. Relatively speaking though, how many others have seen this ncube.com article. How many others have the drive and will pull all-nighters, when others have gone to visit the land of nod? How many others will stop to consider the application of theoretical knowledge, to improve the ‘User Experience’ to ensure users aren’t scrolling endlessly through gallery photos (if you’re visiting this site on a tablet or smartphone – you’re welcome!)? The answer – who knows.

But I’m glad to be entering a community where everyone is looking out for one another. A community which will point out you typed in ‘25rem’ instead of ‘25px’ – although if you didn’t see that, you may have some underlying issues.
