New Zealand Really Isn't That PrettyBlog Post Heading

Clearing immigration at Auckland international Airport seemed like a never-ending episode of Border Police. People who speak little to no English. People who have lost passports. People who have decided to bring every prohibited item on the quarantine list, deciding the best plan is to wear the shoes they soiled with animal faeces from a farm where it’s likely you’ll catch some air-borne virus, whilst lighting a locally rolled cigar. No, I’m not speaking from experience. Partly (cough-cough Myanmar).

Fortunately, my case was fairly cut and dry. I was venturing to New Zealand on a Working Holiday Visa in a hope that the Dental Technology qualification I had graduated with four months prior, would be put to good use. Little did I know, I would end up applying to over 25 farm assistant positions, cupping 1500 cows a day. I didn’t really have any particular ideas regarding the term ‘cupping’, but my first thoughts weren’t “Oh that sticks onto a cow”. Nevertheless, a quick albeit too-vivid-for-10am, YouTube video clarified all I needed to know.

Between you and I, dear reader, being covered daily in a slew of bovine faecal matter fresh from the orifice it emanates, was not the reason I decided to leave Omarama. A particularly stunning township in the South Island. No. It was actually due to the Intercity coach journey, which stopped, at least, every two and a half hours, kept tightly to schedule with endless sigh-inducing one-liners from the driver.

What is the bluest object you can imagine? No, not Eeyore, not that type of blue. I mean the colour. Ok, well I’m about to tell you what is. Lake Tekapo. To say I shed a tear, would be to lie. I sat under a tree, in the shade, and stared out – mesmerised. That memory sticks with me. Along with the two horses I witnessed from the side of State Highway 8 (whilst I was out picking apples - as you do) prancing in a field at sunset. If those two horses weren’t happiness, then nothing else is. I didn’t take a photo of either, so there’s no proof. Apart from my memory. I assure you, NatGeo would pay top-dollar for it.

Ok, so two memories, and disdain of cowshit. What’s the point? Well I’ll tell you. Against my often over-bearing instinct to work hard and play much, much later, I listened to gut instinct (and Google) when I typed ‘What to see in New Zealand’. I was bowled over. I hadn’t actually stopped to consider the monumental move across the world I’d just made. This country, steeped in culture, ingrained into everyday life, from road-signs to greeting anyone with Kia Ora (ignore the ‘a’ in Kia when you say it). Those two memories were just the tip of the iceberg.

If somebody said to me tomorrow, “New Zealand really isn’t that pretty”, I’d tell them to go back and try again. Cape Reinga in the north island, with its endless summer climate, to Milford Sound in the south island, with frightening mountainous peaks everywhere, and all the waterfalls (I do go chasing waterfalls, sorry TLC) and people in-between – if you haven’t experienced that, you haven’t seen the magnificence of New Zealand.
